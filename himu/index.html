Credit/Debt Issues

We work to improve YOUR credit score by using items on YOUR credit report which YOU believe to be inaccurate, misleading, or unverifiable. We work in a serious attempt to resolve all credit disputes. Correcting questionable items which are lowering YOUR credit score is a critical step toward improving YOUR credit score. A clean credit report can mean a higher credit score, lower interest rates and a better lifestyle.

The Fair Credit Reporting Act (“FCRA”), which is encoded in 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq., is federal legislation enacted to promote the accuracy, fairness, and privacy in consumer credit reports. The California Consumer Credit Reporting Agencies Act (“CCRAA”) is the California version of the FCRA. The purpose of the FCRA and the CCRAA is to protect people from the transmission of inaccurate information about them and to establish credit reporting practices that utilize accurate, relevant, and current information in a confidential and responsible manner. We will use these laws to enforce your rights.

Unwanted Phone Calls

If you have received unwanted calls or text messages from creditors, debt collectors, telemarketers, or advertisers, you may have a claim for money damages under the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA). The TCPA, which is encoded in 47 U.S.C. § 227 et seq., is a federal legislation dealing with restrictions on use of telephone equipment, such as an automatic telephone dialing system (“ATDS”).

The purpose of the TCPA is to prohibit the use of ATDS to communicate with others by telephone in a manner that would be an invasion of privacy. It is unlawful for any person to make any calls to a cellular telephone (other than a call made for emergency purposes or made with the prior express consent of the called party) using any ATDS or an artificial or prerecorded voice. The TCPA also restricts telemarketers to call a person’s home phone, via an ATDS or an artificial or prerecorded voice, without prior express consent. Creditors, debt collectors, telemarketers, and advertisers often violate the TCPA.
